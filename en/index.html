Chioggia: The Little Venezia 

Chioggia, known as “Little Venice,” is a beautiful town located in the southern part of the Venetian Lagoon. This charming fishing town is characterized by elegant buildings overlooking picturesque canals, interconnected by numerous bridges. Its narrow streets and bustling fish markets add an authentic and lively touch to the atmosphere.

Riva Vena and Corso del Popolo

Just a short walk from the property, you can enjoy the best bacari, savoring fresh fish paired with fine wine or the typical Spritz. If you prefer a leisurely stroll through the historic center, explore the many shops and indulge in the famous Chioggia creams. Don't miss the "Corso del Popolo," which comes alive every Thursday morning with the weekly market.

Sottomarina

Sottomarina, located just a short distance from Chioggia, is a lively seaside resort known for its long stretch of fine sandy beach and vibrant nightlife. Perfect for those seeking relaxation during the day and fun in the evening, Sottomarina offers numerous venues, restaurants, and bars that enliven the summer scene.

Near Chioggia

Chioggia is surrounded by a variety of fascinating places worth exploring. To the north, there is Pellestrina, a long and narrow island that offers tranquil beaches and breathtaking views of the lagoon. Continuing along, you reach the Lido of Venice, famous for its golden beaches and the prestigious Venice Film Festival. Further on is Venice itself, the city of canals and bridges with its rich history and culture. To the south, the Po Delta offers a unique natural landscape, with an extensive network of canals, lagoons, and rich biodiversity. These are just some of the places, each with its own unique characteristics, making Chioggia an ideal starting point to discover the beauty and diversity of the Veneto region.
